## 1. What is GT3N Forge

GT3N Forge is a gamified open-source builder program operated by GT3N Industries ("GT3N"). Forge provides a platform where participants ("Builders") voluntarily contribute open-source work toward real-world missions that benefit GT3N's portfolio of projects (GOGSverse, REKORD, ARK, HERMES, PICASSO, SMARTCoins ATM, and GT3N Forge itself).

Builders earn **recognition, verified contribution records on the GT3N Wall of Records, gamified rank progression, and eligibility for future economic participation** through their voluntary contributions.

## 2. Nature of Participation

### 2.1 What This Is

GT3N Forge is an **open-source contributor program** operated by a for-profit company. This is the same model used by Google (Android, Kubernetes), Meta (React, PyTorch), Microsoft (VS Code, TypeScript), Vercel (Next.js), Supabase, Discourse, HashiCorp, and many other for-profit technology companies that maintain open-source projects.

Builders contribute voluntarily to open-source missions. All approved contributions are released under permissive open-source licenses (Apache 2.0 for code, CC-BY 4.0 for design/content/research). Outputs are public goods that GT3N -- and anyone else -- may use.

### 2.2 What This Is NOT

Participation in GT3N Forge **does not** create any of the following relationships:

- **Employment.** Builders are not employees of GT3N Industries. GT3N does not provide wages, salaries, benefits, insurance, tax withholding, or any form of employment compensation. No W-2 or 1099 is issued for recognition-based participation in Phase 1-3.
- **Independent contractor engagement.** In Phase 1-3, there is no service agreement, statement of work, or payment-based contractor relationship. (This may change in future phases -- see Section 6.)
- **Partnership or joint venture.** Builders do not hold equity, ownership, or decision-making authority in GT3N Industries by virtue of participation.
- **Securities offering.** Forge is not a securities offering. Future tokenization (Section 6) will only occur with proper legal structure and is not promised, guaranteed, or solicited through current participation.

### 2.3 Builder Autonomy

Builders maintain full autonomy over:

- **Whether to participate.** Participation is entirely voluntary. Builders may join or leave at any time without notice or penalty.
- **Which missions to accept.** No mission is mandatory.
- **When to work.** No required hours, schedules, shifts, or deadlines.
- **How to work.** Builders select their own tools, methods, AI assistants, and workflows.
- **Where to work.** No location requirement.
- **Whether to contribute.** Even after claiming a mission, builders may release the claim at any time without consequence.

By submitting work to GT3N Forge, Builders agree that their contribution will be released under the applicable open-source license. **Builders retain copyright** to their work and grant GT3N (and the public) the rights specified in the applicable license.

### 3.2 Contributor License Agreement (CLA)

Builders sign a one-time Contributor License Agreement (CLA) when joining Forge. The CLA:

- Confirms that the Builder has the right to submit the contributions
- Grants GT3N (and any downstream users) a perpetual, irrevocable license to use the contributions under the applicable open-source license
- Does NOT transfer copyright -- Builder retains ownership
- Does NOT grant GT3N exclusive rights -- contributions remain public goods

The CLA is based on the **Apache Software Foundation Individual Contributor License Agreement template** (industry-standard, widely adopted).

### 3.3 Builder Portfolio Rights

Builders retain the right to:

- Reference their Forge contributions in personal portfolios, resumes, and professional profiles
- Display their public Builder profile and Wall of Records entries
- Use their own contributions in other projects (subject to the open-source license terms)
- Describe the work they performed

### 3.4 GT3N's Use of Contributions

GT3N Industries may use Builder contributions:

- In its commercial products (GOGSverse, REKORD, ARK, HERMES, PICASSO, etc.)
- As components of paid services
- In marketing, demonstrations, and public materials
- For any purpose permitted by the applicable open-source license

This is the same right anyone else (including competitors, students, other organizations) has to use these contributions. GT3N's use is non-exclusive.

## 4. What Builders Receive

### 4.1 Recognition and Verification

Upon successful completion and approval of missions, Builders receive:

| Reward | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Wall of Records Entry** | A permanent, public, verifiable record of the contribution. Each entry includes the SHA-256 hash of the output, a chain link to the Builder's previous contribution, mission and project attribution, date, and reviewer notes. |
| **Experience Points (XP)** | A numerical measure of verified contribution volume and difficulty. XP is non-transferable and has no cash value in Phase 1-3. |
| **Rank Progression** | Apprentice -> Builder -> Maker -> Architect -> Core Contributor. Higher ranks unlock more complex missions and leadership opportunities. |
| **Reputation Score** | Quality metric calculated from approval rates weighted by mission difficulty. |
| **Badges** | Achievement markers for specific accomplishments (Polymath, GOGSverse Champion, Founder Builder, etc.). |
| **Public Builder Profile** | A profile page displaying rank, XP, reputation, project contributions, badges, and verified Wall of Records history. Designed to function as a professional portfolio. |
| **Founder Builder Status** | Available to early-phase contributors (Phase 1-2) -- a permanent, non-transferable badge recognizing participation before the platform opened publicly. |

### 4.2 Why the Wall of Records Matters

The Wall of Records is GT3N Forge's permanent recognition layer:

- **Public:** Anyone can browse and verify
- **Permanent:** Records are never deleted, even if a Builder leaves
- **Verifiable:** Anyone can re-hash a contribution's output and confirm it matches the recorded hash
- **Cryptographically chained:** Each Builder's contributions form a SHA-256 hash chain -- if any record is tampered with, the chain breaks
- **REKORD-anchored:** Wall of Records entries will sync to REKORD (Phase 3) for institutional-grade preservation and search

A Wall of Records entry is more verifiable proof of work than any letter of recommendation, certificate, or unverified portfolio claim.

### 4.3 What Builders Do NOT Receive in Phase 1-3

- No wages, salary, or hourly compensation
- No benefits (health insurance, retirement, paid time off)
- No tax documents (W-2, 1099) for participation
- No equity or ownership in GT3N Industries
- No guaranteed minimum compensation of any kind
- No specific or guaranteed token or financial reward

## 5. Missions and Submissions

### 5.1 Mission Structure

Missions are posted on the GT3N Forge platform. Each mission specifies:

- The GT3N project it serves
- Mission type (code, design, content, research, ops)
- Difficulty level (Starter, Standard, Advanced, Elite)
- XP reward
- Required rank and reputation thresholds
- Deliverables and acceptance criteria
- Open-source license that will apply to the output
- Validation tier (Tier 1: Checklist, Tier 2: Quality Review, Tier 3: Expert Review)

### 5.2 Submission Requirements

All submissions must include:

- Link to the deliverable output
- **Process notes** (minimum 50 words) describing how the work was completed
- **AI disclosure** stating what AI tools were used and how
- Output files as specified by the mission

### 5.3 AI Tool Usage

Builders may use any AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, Midjourney, local models, etc.). AI usage is permitted and encouraged where appropriate.

**AI disclosure is mandatory.** Every submission must include:

- Which AI tools were used
- What they were used for
- What portions of the work were completed manually
- Self-reported AI percentage (0-100%)

AI disclosure is a transparency mechanism, not a penalty. Builders are evaluated on the quality and honesty of their output and process, not on whether AI was used.

### 5.4 Validation

Submissions are reviewed according to the mission's specified validation tier:

- **Approved** submissions earn XP and are recorded on the Wall of Records under the applicable open-source license.
- **Rejected** submissions earn no XP. Builders receive written feedback.
- **Revision requested** submissions may be revised and resubmitted once.

## 6. Future Phases and Tokenization

### 6.1 Stated Intent (Not a Promise)

GT3N Industries' stated intent is to expand GT3N Forge across multiple phases:

- **Phase 1-2 (Current):** Open-source contributor program, no payment
- **Phase 3:** REKORD integration (contributions sync to GT3N's Knowledge-as-a-Service platform)
- **Phase 4:** Add paid contractor missions for proprietary work (when GT3N has revenue or grant funding)
- **Phase 5+:** Introduce token economy via SMARTCoins ATM, with proper legal infrastructure

### 6.2 Retroactive Recognition for Early Contributors

When (and if) a token economy launches in Phase 5 or beyond, GT3N's stated intent is to provide **retroactive token allocation** to all Wall of Records contributors based on their contribution history, weighted by:

- XP earned
- Mission difficulty
- Phase of participation (early contributors receive amplified recognition)
- Reputation score

**This is a stated intent, not a guarantee.** Token launches require:

- Mature legal infrastructure (securities counsel)
- Regulatory compliance
- Functional SMARTCoins ATM infrastructure
- Market conditions
- Possibly years of development

Contributors participating in Phase 1-3 do so based on the immediate value of open-source contribution and Wall of Records recognition. Future tokens, if launched, are additive recognition -- **not deferred wages or compensation for current work**.

### 6.3 No Securities Offering

This document, the Forge platform, the Wall of Records, and all related communications do not constitute an offer of securities, an investment opportunity, or a solicitation of any kind. Any future token offering would be conducted under appropriate legal structure, with all required disclosures, and would be a separate event from current Forge participation.

## 7. Conduct and Community Standards

### 7.1 Expected Conduct

Builders are expected to:

- Submit honest, original work
- Provide truthful AI disclosure
- Treat other Builders and reviewers with respect
- Provide constructive feedback when reviewing others' work
- Report bugs, issues, or concerns through appropriate channels
- Abide by the GT3N Forge Code of Conduct (based on Contributor Covenant)

### 7.2 Prohibited Conduct

The following may result in account suspension or removal:

- Submitting plagiarized, copied, or fraudulent work
- Deliberately dishonest AI disclosure
- Creating multiple accounts (sybil behavior)
- Colluding with reviewers to approve low-quality work
- Harassing, threatening, or abusing other participants
- Attempting to exploit or manipulate the XP, rank, or reputation systems
- Violating the open-source license terms

### 7.3 Enforcement

GT3N reserves the right to suspend or remove any Builder's account for violation of these terms. Decisions are made at GT3N's discretion. Builders may appeal by contacting GT3N directly.

## 8. Account and Data

### 8.1 Account Termination

Builders may close their account at any time. Upon closure:

- Active claims are released
- The Builder's profile is deactivated
- **Wall of Records entries remain permanently** (they are part of the immutable record and cannot be selectively removed -- this is essential to the integrity of the chain)
- Open-source contributions remain under their applicable licenses (cannot be retroactively withdrawn)

### 8.2 Data Retention

GT3N retains:

- Wall of Records entries (permanently)
- Submission metadata (hashes, timestamps, reviewer decisions)
- Builder profile information (handle, email, track, rank, XP)

GT3N does not sell Builder personal information to third parties.
